What is this section for?
The Week 1 Decision Checkpoint is a go/no-go decision tool for each of your 7 audience sets.

After 7 days, you will have enough data to decide which sets deserve more budget and which should be paused. The goal is to identify your top 3 performers and reallocate budget from the bottom 4.

How to use CPM vs Benchmark: CPM tells you how expensive it is for Meta to show your ad 1,000 times. Your location has a known CPM range from your account history. If your CPM is higher than the benchmark, it means the auction is more competitive than usual — either your audience is too narrow, your creative relevance score is low, or there's a seasonal/competitive spike. If your CPM is lower than benchmark, Meta is delivering efficiently — good signal.

You don't need a perfect CPM. You need a good Cost Per ThruPlay. CPM is context. Cost Per ThruPlay is the decision metric.

Budget Reallocation Rule

Red Flag Protocol — Act Immediately, Don't Wait for Weekly Review
These situations require same-day action. Do not wait for your weekly review slot.
🔴
Cost Per ThruPlay doubles overnight
Check if a new ad was added or budget was changed yesterday. Revert the change immediately.
🔴
An ad set stops spending entirely
Check audience size (too small?), budget level, and ad approval status. Check for policy violations.
🔴
Frequency jumps above 4.0 in a single day
Add frequency cap immediately (2 per 7 days). Your audience is too small for the budget level.
🟡
Meta sends a "Learning Limited" notification
Consolidate ads to 3–5 per ad set. Consider increasing budget. Do not split test during this period.
🔴
ThruPlay Rate drops below 5% across all ads
Pause all ads immediately. Upload fresh creatives before resuming. Do not keep spending on a failing hook.
🟡
CPM rises 30%+ in 2 consecutive days with no changes made
Check for seasonal events or competitor activity. Consider pausing and resuming after 48 hours to reset auction position.
